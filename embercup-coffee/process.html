The roast

Twelve minutes, tracked to the second.

Here's what happens to green coffee between the drum and the bag — the same five stages, every batch, tuned differently per bean.

0:00 — 160°C

Charge

Green beans hit the drum. Moisture starts evaporating immediately — this stage decides how evenly the rest of the roast will develop.

4:30 — 175°C

Yellowing

Beans lose their green color and start smelling like toast and hay. Sugars begin to caramelize under the surface.

8:00 — 196°C

First crack

An audible crack as internal steam pressure splits the bean structure. This is the earliest point coffee becomes drinkable.

9:40 — 204°C

Development

The window between first crack and drop — where roast level is decided. Seconds here change the entire flavor profile.

11:40 — Drop

Cooling

Beans are dropped into a cooling tray and air-cooled in under 4 minutes to lock the profile in place.

Quality control

We cup every batch, twice.

Each roast is cupped immediately after resting, then again 5 days later once degassing settles. Anything that doesn't hold its profile gets pulled before it reaches a bag.

DAY 1

Rest cupping

First taste, 24 hours post-roast — checking for defects and roast evenness.

DAY 5

Final cupping

Full flavor profile confirmed once CO2 degassing stabilizes the cup.
