About Mezbaan

A kitchen that never learned how to rush.

From one tandoor on Circular Road to the table you're reading this from — here's how Mezbaan came to be.

How We Started

It began with four tables and a mother's recipe book

In 2016, Mubashir's grandmother's recipe notebook came out of a kitchen drawer in Model Town and turned into a menu. Mezbaan opened with four tables, one tandoor, and a simple rule: cook for the guest in front of you the way you'd cook for family.

Ten years on, the tables have multiplied and the dining room has grown, but the rule hasn't moved an inch. Every biryani is still built to order. Every naan still comes out of the tandoor blistered and warm. Every guest still gets asked twice if they've had enough to eat.

What We Stand For

The four rules the kitchen never breaks

Slow, on Purpose

Biryani takes six hours. Nihari cooks overnight. We'd rather make you wait than make it wrong.

Sourced Nearby

Meat, dairy, and produce come from farms within the district, delivered fresh before every service.

Family Seating

Our largest tables sit ten. Bring the whole family — we'll pull the chairs together.

Rooted in Bahawalpur

One location, one kitchen, one team who knows most of our regulars by name.

The People Behind It

Run by a kitchen crew who started as guests

Half of our current cooks first walked through the door as customers. Our head chef, who trained under the founder's mother, still tastes every batch of karahi masala before it touches the stove.

We're a small, close team — front of house, kitchen, and delivery riders who all answer to the same standard: would you serve this to your own family?
